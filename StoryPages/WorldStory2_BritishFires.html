British Wildfire Wreaks Havoc

A large wildfire has erupted in southern England, and officials are scrambling to respond. This is the largest wildfire in England on record, and even larger ones may erupt in the future.

The wildfire is burning in Thetford Forest, a large forest situated northeast of Cambridge, England. It is not known how the fire was started, but according to the local fire department chief, it is “bigger than anything we’ve faced before.” The fire is exacerbated partly because of the dryness in the area. At this time, England has been very dry for many years, with little rain falling, and the dryness approaching the level of a drought.

Before this, there have been smaller wildfires in England, but they were put out relatively quickly. However, with the dryness increasing, the fires have grown worse and worse, eventually culminating in this massive blaze. “It’s never been this bad,” says a local firefighter, John White. “The fires are getting bigger, and my job is getting more and more dangerous.”

In fact, more than just the firefighters are in danger. The surrounding residents, usually unaffected by wildfires, have been forced to evacuate as a result of the latest fire. Those that chose to stay are “fire-proofing” their homes.

William Billington is one resident who chose to stay. “I can’t leave,” he stated. “All my family is here, and we’ve lived here for the past 50 years.” Billington fire-proofed his home by clearing vegetation around it, and by applying fire-resistant material to the roof and sides of the house to protect it. “I’ll try to outlast it,” he told Betralund News.

But while some residents stay, others are leaving, such as Felicia Chathton, who lives with her aging mother in Mildenhall, which is southwest of Thetford Forest. “I had to leave,” she stated. “I didn’t want to, but my mother is living with me, and so I had to evacuate to keep her safe.” She is evacuating to nearby Cambridge, where she will be staying with relatives.

As residents move away from the blaze, firefighting teams are moving toward it. Government officials are coordinating a massive offensive, bringing firefighters from all over England to fight the blaze. But the fire teams will have some learning to do first.

“England has never had wildfires of this scale,” said government official Charles Polington, who is in charge of the efforts. “Our firefighters are experienced with house fires and small brush fires, but not with fires of this magnitude. These fires are much larger, difficult to contain, and require much different tactics.”

However, as firefighters in England struggle to contain the blaze in Thetford Forest, they remain committed to winning the fight. “We need to get people out, contain the fire, put it out, and then clean up the damage,” Polington stated confidently. “And the next time a fire like this starts, we’ll be ready.”
