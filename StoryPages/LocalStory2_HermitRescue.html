Hermit Saves Drowning Tourist, Advocates for Betralund's Forests

Last week, a hermit saved a tourist from drowning after she fell of a cliff trying to take a selfie. This comes as tourism has increased more than seventy percent in the past year.

Christian Olavson, a self-proclaimed hermit who lives alone near lake Duptvan, told reporters that he was walking along the lake when he heard cries for help. “I heard someone splashing and yelling,” he said, “I ran to the lake’s edge, and it was a woman splashing around, she looked like she couldn’t swim.”

Olavson jumped into the freezing water, and dragged the woman to shore, where he found she was unconscious. He performed CPR to resuscitate her, and after fifteen minutes she woke up. “It was terrifying,” he said. “I was partly in shock from the cold, and I was trying to get her to wake up, and I thought she might be dead,” he told reporters.

The woman, Laura Shingle, was part of a tour group hiking along Lake Duptvan. Shingle told reporters that she fell in when she stayed behind the group to take a selfie on the edge of a cliff, which, in hindsight, “may not have been a good idea.” According to her, she was leaning over the cliff, trying to get a perfect shot, then overbalanced and fell into the freezing water, which at this time of year is around 5° C.

Shingle told reporters. “I didn’t know how to swim, and the water was so cold, and I guess I was just panicking,” she said. “I was yelling for help, but nobody was there, and I was getting weaker, and my phone was all wet and useless. I must’ve passed out, because the next thing I remembered was being on the shore.”

And while her rescuer, Christian Olavson, is being hailed as a hero, he is also using this attention to speak out. He is frustrated with the noise and littering the large numbers of tourists cause. “There was never any plastic or bottles or anything before they started coming, but now I find a piece of trash every few feet," he complains.

The newly formed Department of Tourism is listening to his statements. It announced that it planned to limit the number of tourists allowed on each trip, and they also plan to set up a system of forest rangers to protect the natural areas and the tourists from incidents like this one. And if anyone ever falls into Lake Duptvan again, someone will definitely be there to help.
