<h2>Opinion: Betralund Should Stand up to Russia in this Trade War</h2>
<p>As tensions rise over the iron trade between Russia and Betralund, negotiators from both sides plan to meet in Moscow this week.</p>
<p> If negotiations fail, Betralund could lose its main source of iron, used for construction and other projects. But that doesn’t matter. I believe we shouldn’t be negotiating with Russia at all, but standing up for ourselves in this trade war.</p>
<p>Betralund has very few natural sources of iron, and is forced to import it from various global sources, the primary source being Russia. Last week, Russia announced that it was increasing the price of its iron by 20%. Betralund contested the price raise, and Russia responded by threatening to stop all iron trade with Betralund. As mentioned, Betralund is planning to send negotiators to Russia to get a better deal.</p>
<p>I believe that we shouldn’t negotiate with Russia, but instead stand up for ourselves. Our country is young in the global space, and we can’t let ourselves be trampled on. We need to stand up to ourselves if we want to survive in the global trade environment.</p>
<p>I think that in response to Russia, we can threaten to cut off some goods of our own. We are the cheapest supply of dairy and dairy products to Russia, and if we stopped trading with them, they would be forced to trade with other countries, who have significantly higher prices for these products. Thus, this threat can be used as a weapon in this war.</p>
<p>Additionally, we can survive without iron from Russia. We can obtain iron from other sources, such as Europe or the United States, which will also allow us to build strong alliances with those nations. We can also use sustainable building materials, such as wood, to replace the use of iron in many areas.</p>
<p>In conclusion, Betralund needs to be strong in this trade dispute with Russia. We can stand up and threaten our own retaliations if Russia continues its threats. Besides this, we can easily survive without iron from Russia, by using other materials, and obtaining the resource from other sources.</p>
